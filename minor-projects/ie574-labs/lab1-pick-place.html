What Did I Do?

For this project, I programmed a robot called Sawyer to automatically pick up colored blocks and place them in the correct bins. This involved writing instructions that tell the robot:

  • Where each block is located
  • How to move its arm to reach the block safely
  • When to open and close its gripper (the hand that grabs things)
  • Where to place each block
Goal: Successfully move all 6 blocks into their matching color bins without dropping them or hitting anything.

How It Works

The robot follows a simple step-by-step process for each block:

  1. Move Above: The robot moves its arm above the block
  2. Move Down: It lowers the arm to the block's location
  3. Grab: The gripper closes and picks up the block
  4. Move Up: The arm rises to avoid hitting anything
  5. Move to Bin: The arm travels to the correct color bin
  6. Release: The gripper opens and the block drops into the bin

This process is repeated for all 6 blocks, and the robot sorts them correctly into red, green, and blue bins.
