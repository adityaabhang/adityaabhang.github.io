Overview

This lab covered trajectory planning for robotic manipulators in both joint space and Cartesian space. I implemented trapezoidal and polynomial velocity profiles to generate smooth motion between waypoints, ensuring the robot moved without abrupt acceleration changes. The lab bridged the gap between knowing where the robot needs to go and generating the actual motion commands to get there.

Methodology

I implemented cubic and quintic polynomial interpolation for joint-space trajectories, designed trapezoidal velocity profiles with acceleration limits, and computed Cartesian straight-line paths using inverse kinematics at each interpolation step. Each approach was evaluated for smoothness, execution time, and joint-limit compliance.
